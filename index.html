Who in the world am I and who do I think I am?

Hello, my name is Aidan Domenis and these are some things you should know about me. I’m a first year Interactive Media student at Confederation college and I love the program. Although this is my first year at the college, I spent a year at Lakehead University in the Visual Arts program where I developed my skills as a painter, sculptor, drawer, potter, print-maker, etc. Going into my first year of post-secondary, I had no idea where I wanted to end up and even though the program was amazing, I felt sort of lost. I always wondered how I would use the skills I was learning in a more practical way, and that’s when I discovered my love for graphic design. Half way through the following summer, I knew I needed to change my program. It wasn’t until the week before school started that I settled on the IMD program at the college. Fortunately, it took me exactly where I needed to go and opened up so many doors for me. Before this program, I was not much of a photography bluff, and I still wouldn’t consider myself a photographer. However, I have learned many useful techniques and most of all, how to even work a standard DSLR camera. Despite how simple a camera seems, there is so much to know about them and I learned that pretty quickly. As I got comfortable with my camera, I realized that I enjoy taking pictures with high contrast and bold colours. Simply taking a picture is fun, but I enjoy the technical side where you get to play with lighting, depth of field, shadows, and visual effects. Because I am a musician and I love 60s and 70s culture, especially music, my favourite photographer at the moment is Mick Rock. His colourful and high-contrast photos of rock legends such as David Bowie, Queen, Iggy Pop, and the Ramones have an inspiration on the types of photos I enjoy taking, especially in studio. After I graduate from the IMD program, I plan to move away from Thunder Bay for a period of time to explore different options. As for photography, I see it as more of a hobby and a skill than an occupation. That being said, I have no idea what road I will take next, but I’m eager to find out.
